Time to get serious. My academic interests include high-performance computing, visualization, computational geometry, geometric modeling and computational sciences, specifically in numerical methods, optimization and statistical methods.

I am particularly exploring medical applications. Though technology drives today's medical industry, it still largely remains technologically untapped, distinctly in third world countries like India. A lot of imaging and geometric processing is used for quantitative diagnosis in clinical practices. Commonly in the form of MRI, CT scan, x-ray and less commonly in deep brain stimulation, atrial fibrillation. I am interested in setting up simple and reliable DICOM images database and web-service all the way to solving distributed and parallel algorithm challenges involved in image data visualization and analysis. I want to build applications which respond real-time to the medical data of a patient, eliminating heavy costs of bills incurred today. Importantly, emerging technologies in computer hardware, middleware and APIs are driving the progress in the grid and/or ubiquitous computing technologies. If you are interested in current collaborative works in medical and computing fields, Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute at the University of Utah is a good place to start.

Mathematics is love, hence is machine learning (ML). But I don't have any prior experience in ML. Shortly, I plan to start a couple of courses focused on statistical methods for machine learning and data analysis.
